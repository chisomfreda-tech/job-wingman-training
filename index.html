Work Authorization & Visa Sponsorship

The critical knowledge for helping international clients find sponsoring employers.

Why This Matters

Many of our clients need visa sponsorship. This is often the #1 filter that determines which jobs they can even apply to.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Understanding

Not every company sponsors visas. Not every role qualifies. Your job is to find the intersection of: roles that fit the client's skills + companies likely to sponsor + roles that qualify for sponsorship.

üá∫üá∏ US Work Authorization

Common Visa Types

VisaWhat It IsKey Details
H-1BSpecialty occupation visaRequires bachelor's degree, lottery system (March), 3+3 years
OPTPost-graduation work permit12 months (+ 24 months for STEM), no lottery needed
STEM OPTOPT extension for STEM graduatesE-Verify employer required, 24 extra months
L-1Intra-company transferFor employees transferring within same company
Green CardPermanent residencyLong process, employer-sponsored (EB-2, EB-3) or family

How to Research Sponsorship History

  1. MyVisaJobs.com ‚Äî Search company H-1B filings history
  2. H1Bdata.info ‚Äî Database of H-1B applications
  3. Company careers page ‚Äî Some explicitly state sponsorship policy
  4. Job description ‚Äî Look for "must be authorized to work" (usually means NO sponsorship)

üîç Signals: Will They Sponsor?

‚úÖ Likely to Sponsor
  • Large tech companies (FAANG, etc.)
  • Big 4 consulting firms
  • Major banks & financial institutions
  • Companies with H-1B history
  • University/research positions
‚ùå Unlikely to Sponsor
  • Small companies (<50 employees)
  • Startups (unless well-funded)
  • Government contractors
  • Companies with no H-1B history
  • Roles saying "must be authorized"

üá¨üáß UK Work Authorization

Skilled Worker Visa Requirements

  1. Licensed Sponsor: Employer must hold a valid sponsor license
  2. Certificate of Sponsorship: Employer issues CoS for specific role
  3. Salary Threshold: Minimum ¬£38,700/year (or lower for certain roles)
üîç How to Check UK Sponsor Status
  1. Go to: gov.uk Register of Licensed Sponsors
  2. Download the current sponsor list (Excel file)
  3. Search for the company name
  4. If listed with "Worker" license = they CAN sponsor

Using Simplify

Master the autofill tool that powers your application workflow.

What is Simplify?

Simplify is a browser extension that automatically fills out job applications. It saves client information and applies it across different application forms, dramatically speeding up the application process.

Extension Link: Install Simplify from Chrome Web Store

üí° Why We Use Simplify
  • Fill applications in 30 seconds instead of 10 minutes
  • Consistent information across all applications
  • Tracks applied jobs automatically
  • Reduces errors from manual entry

üö® CRITICAL: One Chrome Profile Per Client

Before anything else, you MUST create a separate Google Chrome profile for EACH client. This keeps everything separate and prevents mixing up client data.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why This Matters

If you use one profile for multiple clients, Simplify will autofill the WRONG client's information. This is a serious error that wastes applications and breaks client trust.

1

Open Chrome Profile Manager

  • Click on your profile icon in the top-right corner of Chrome (circle with your initial or photo)
  • Click "Add" or "+ Add" at the bottom of the dropdown
2

Create New Profile

  • Choose "Continue without an account" (you don't need to sign into Google)
  • Name the profile with the client's name: e.g., "Client - John Smith"
  • Pick a color/icon so it's easy to identify
  • Click "Done"
3

Install Simplify in This Profile

  • In the NEW profile window, go to Simplify Extension
  • Click "Add to Chrome"
  • Pin the extension to your toolbar for easy access
4

Set Up Client's Simplify Account

  • Click the Simplify icon ‚Üí Create account or sign in
  • Fill out the profile completely with the CLIENT's information
  • Upload the client's current resume (PDF)
  • Fill in work history, education, skills, contact info
‚ö†Ô∏è Repeat for Every New Client

Every time you get a new client, create a new Chrome profile and set up Simplify fresh. This takes 10 minutes upfront but prevents costly mistakes.

  1. Open job posting ‚Äî Navigate to the application page
  2. Click Simplify icon ‚Äî Extension detects the form
  3. Click "Autofill" ‚Äî Simplify fills the form
  4. Review EVERY field ‚Äî Check for errors or missed fields

Autofill isn't perfect. Common issues: wrong work authorization answer, missing phone format, custom questions left blank. Always review before submitting.

Company Research

Verify company fit, sponsorship likelihood, and avoid wasting client applications.

Why Research Matters

Not all companies are equal. A great job title at a bad company is still a bad job. Company research helps you:

  • Verify the company is legitimate
  • Check if they'll sponsor visas (if client needs it)
  • Identify mission-driven organizations (if client wants it)
  • Avoid red flag companies

Identifying Mission-Driven Organizations

When clients want "social impact" or "mission-driven" roles, they mean specific types of organizations:

‚úÖ Legitimate Social-Cause Organizations

  • 501(c)(3) Nonprofits: Search on GuideStar/Candid
  • B-Corps: Certified benefit corporations ‚Äî B-Corp Directory
  • Social Enterprises: For-profit with social mission central to business
  • NGOs: Non-governmental organizations

‚ùå NOT Social-Cause (Common Mistakes)

  • Regular tech companies with a "mission statement"
  • Job boards or recruiting platforms
  • Companies that donate to charity but aren't mission-driven
  • "Impact" investors that are primarily profit-focused
‚ö†Ô∏è Key Distinction

A company having a nice-sounding mission statement doesn't make it mission-driven. Google's mission is "to organize the world's information" ‚Äî that doesn't make it a nonprofit.

Where to Find Social-Cause Jobs

  • Idealist ‚Äî idealist.org
  • Work For Good ‚Äî workforgood.org
  • B Work ‚Äî bwork.com (B-Corp jobs)
  • 80,000 Hours ‚Äî 80000hours.org/job-board

Researching Visa Sponsorship History

For US (H-1B)

  • MyVisaJobs.com ‚Äî Search company H-1B filing history
  • H1Bdata.info ‚Äî Detailed salary and job title data

For UK

  • UK Sponsor Register ‚Äî Download and search the Excel file

For Canada

  • Job Bank Canada ‚Äî Shows LMIA-approved postings

‚úì Quick Company Research Checklist

‚òê Company exists and is legitimate (not a scam)

‚òê Company size/stage matches client preferences

‚òê If client needs visa: Check sponsorship history

‚òê If client wants mission-driven: Verify it's actually nonprofit/social enterprise

‚òê No recent major layoffs or scandals (quick Google)

Module 12

Quality Control & Common Mistakes

The final checkpoint ‚Äî learn to catch errors before they reach clients.

The QC Mindset

Quality control isn't a final step ‚Äî it's a continuous practice. Every job you add should pass the QC test before you move on.

‚ö†Ô∏è Trust is Fragile

One bad application can damage client trust built over weeks. A role that doesn't match their criteria tells them we're not paying attention.

Common Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

üö® Salary Below Minimum ‚ñº

Prevention: Always check salary before adding. Convert hourly to annual (hourly √ó 2080 = annual). When in doubt, skip the role.

üö® Wrong Work Mode ‚ñº

Prevention: Read the actual job description. Look for "must be in commuting distance" = not actually remote. "Flexible" often means hybrid.

üö® Wrong Location ‚ñº

Prevention: Verify "Remote" isn't "Remote - CA only". Check country ‚Äî UK jobs can appear in US searches.

üö® Seniority Mismatch ‚ñº

Prevention: Read experience requirements. Watch for level indicators: Associate, Junior, Senior, Lead, Director.

üö® Misunderstanding "Mission-Driven" ‚ñº

Prevention: Verify 501(c)(3) status or B-Corp certification. Use dedicated platforms like Idealist. A nice mission statement ‚â† mission-driven.

üö® Stale Postings ‚ñº

Prevention: Always check post date. Stick to jobs posted within 7-14 days. Sort by "Most recent".

‚úì Pre-Add Quality Check (Use for EVERY Job)

‚òê Title fit: Matches client's target or clearly related

‚òê Level appropriate: Entry/mid/senior matches client's experience

‚òê Location correct: Matches preferences (including remote verification)

‚òê Work mode verified: Remote/hybrid/on-site matches client needs

‚òê Salary acceptable: At or above minimum (or "Not listed" but role level suggests OK)

‚òê Skills aligned: Client has most required skills (70%+ rule)

‚òê Posting fresh: Posted within last 7-14 days

‚òê Visa check (if needed): Company sponsors or likely to

‚òê Mission check (if needed): Actually nonprofit/social enterprise

‚òê No red flags: Company is legitimate, no major concerns

When to Skip a Job

It's better to apply to fewer quality roles than many questionable ones. Skip if:

  • Salary is below minimum (hard stop)
  • Location doesn't match and not remote
  • Client clearly lacks most required skills (less than 50% match)
  • Seniority is way off (5+ years difference)
  • Posting is old (30+ days)
  • Company has terrible reviews (Glassdoor below 3.0)
  • Client needs sponsorship and company doesn't sponsor
  • Something feels off ‚Äî trust your judgment
